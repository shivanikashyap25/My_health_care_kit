Immunization

Immunization, also known as vaccination, is a preventive healthcare measure aimed at bolstering the body's immune response to specific infectious agents. It involves administering vaccines containing weakened or inactive forms of pathogens, which stimulate the immune system to produce antibodies.

Types of Immunization

  • Live Attenuated Vaccines
  • Inactivated Vaccines
  • Subunit, Recombinant, Polysaccharide, and Conjugate Vaccines
  • Toxoid Vaccines
  • DNA and RNA Vaccines
  • Passive Immunization
  • Viral Vector Vaccines
